Week 3 of 3-Week Technique Mesocycle

Beginners and Intermediates

Coaches' Notes
  • Last week of introductory mesocycle
Athlete Notes
  • Last week of mostly technical emphasis
  • Timing and precision is important; speed and power will come later.
  • If you do not already have weightlifting shoes, you should get a pair ASAP

Sunday

  1. Pause Front Squat 10 secs 3x3

    • This exercise is primarily to warm up up your bottom position for the next exercise.
    • Perform that descent at a moderate speed, pause 10 secs at bottom, then recover quickly.

  2. Tempo Front Squat 10 secs + 3 bounces 5x3

    • Descend slowly to within a few centimeters of rock bottom (which you should have found in the previous exercise).
    • When you are within a few centimeters of rock bottom, allow your legs to relax suddenly so that you free fall to bottom position (hamstrings on calves), then bounce up.
    • Rather than stand back up immediately out of the bounce, relax, then bounce up to a height less than half way to parallel two more times.
    • On the third bounce, stand back up.
    • 3 bounces + 1 recovery = 1 rep
    • Use only enough weight to find your bounce. This is not a strength or conditioning exercise.

  3. Tempo Halting Snatch Deadlift 10/10/10 5x2

    Find your snatch start position, then stand up until the bar touches your hips. 10 seconds up, 10 seconds pause in halting position, 10 seconds lowering back to start.
    • Your center of gravity should remain constant from start to finish (especially avoid drifting into your heels)
    • The bar should lightly brush your legs from start to finish
    • Your arms and upper back should remain passive the entire movement (it is a dead hang), but you must maintain your back arch and open chest.
    • In the finish (halting) position, your knees should still be slightly bent and the bar should be plumb below the rear of your armpit. The bar should remain touching the articulation point of your hips.

  4. Snatch Grip Push Press Behind the Neck 5x5 w/5 secs hold

    • You may go fairly heavy on this exercise as long as you can hold the weight stable overhead for five seconds on the final rep of each set.
    • If you start to lose your balance or cannot keep your arms locked out for five seconds on the last rep of a set, reduce the weight.

  5. Drop squat warm up (body only) for 30 seconds

    • Try to find the feeling of free-falling all the way to the bottom

  6. Muscle snatch + Overhead Squat + Good morning snatch balance 5x3

    • You should be fairly tired from the push presses so that you will naturally use light weights on this exercise
    • The muscle snatch should be performed with a light touch of your hips (not all muscle snatches are performed like this variation)
    • After recovering from the squat, lower the bar to your back, then comments the G.M.S.B.

  7. Reverse Plank 3x1 minute

Tuesday

  1. Tall Snatch 5x3

    • Start the movement with your heels 1 mm off the ground, quads and glutes flexed as tight as possible until you feel a little burn.
    • Commence the movement by relaxing your knee joint and lifting the point of your elbow directly to the ceiling at the exact same moment.
    • Try to drop to rock bottom without slowing yourself down.
    • It is an error to give yourself even the slightest boost / bounce up when commencing the movement. The purpose of the hard flex is to make it easier to commence the movement by just relaxing your legs like someone secretly pulled a chair out from undernearth you when you started to sit down.

  2. Clean Rack Delivery 4x5

  3. Tall Clean 5x3

    • Commence this movement exactly like the tall snatch.
    • Since your hands start closer together, it is more difficult (less natural) to move your elbows correctly. You have to deliberately move your elbows WIDE and HIGH before you turn over the bar into rack position.
    • The clean is not a fast reverse curl. In a reverse curl, the bar rotates around your body with your elbows locked in place as the fulcrum. In a clean, you lift your elbows high, and *the bar is the fulcrum* as your elbows rotate around the bar, and you make full use of the bearings in the collar.

  4. Press Behind the Next in Jerk Split with Cheat 5x5

    • On each rep, boost the weight overhead with a small jump into the air from split position. The jump should not be so aggressive that you lose your balance and cannot land with your feet in the same split position that you started with.

  5. Drop Split 5x3

    • Not to be confused with the drop jerk.
    • Place the bar on your back, and leave it there for the entire movement; your arms are not involved in this exercise, other than to keep the bar placed on your back as they would when you squat.
    • Start standing tall with your quads and glutes flexed, and heels 1 mm off of the ground.
    • Without boosting yourself up even the tiniest bit, (i.e., no cheating), drop into your jerk split.
    • Add as much weight as you like as you get comfortable, and use a belt if necessary.
    • This is an exercise in speed, timing, and accuracy. Adding weight provides an intimidation factor, but you never actually "lift" the weight. It will, however, be more difficult to stop yourself from going lower in the split.
    • Remember, gravity is a constant, so the speed that you need to move your feet into the split remains the same regardless of the amount of weight you put on your back. If you don't get your feet into the proper split, it is usually because you mis-timed when to start moving them.

  6. Barbell Row 4x8

  7. Banded side plank clamshells 4x10
