Week 2 of Mesocycle 2

General Program

Coaches' Notes
  • If you have athletes that PR during the macrocycle (including at a meet), they should continue to calculate their relative intensities based on their PR's at the beginning of the cycle. The pattern of volume and intensity as the macrocycle progresses already presumes that athletes will improve their strength and conditioning relative to when they started.
  • If you calculate intensities based on a new PR before the macrocyle ends, the athlete will tend to overtrain and regress.
  • New PR's should be used at the start of the next cycle.
Athlete Notes
  • This week has about 20% less volume than last week, but the intensity creeps up a bit.

Sunday

  1. Tall Power Snatch bar(4x3)

    • The purpose is to warm up the correct motor patterns before you add weight.
    • Start standing tall with your heels 1mm off of the ground.
    • Initiate the movement by lifting your elbows aggressively while simultaneously jumping down.
    • While jumping down, slide your feet out into squat catch position.
    • Try to lock out the bar overhead at the same moment your feet reach their final position.
    • This movement requires speed & agility to be performed correctly.

  2. Power Snatch 60x3 65x3 70x3 75x3 80(2x3)

  3. Tall Clean + Tall Jerk bar(4x3)

    • A coach should demo this movement
    • The purpose is to warm up the correct motor patterns before you add weight.

  4. PC+PJ 65x3 70x3 75(2x3) 80(2x3)

  5. Front Squat w/2 bounces 60x3 65x2 70x2 75x2 80x2 83x2

    • Recover on the 2nd bounce

  6. Snatch Pull 85(2x3) 90(2x2)

  7. 4 rounds:

    • Barbell Rows x10
    • Hip Airplane x10

  8. Pinch Grip Plate Holds 3x1 minute or to failure

Tuesday

  1. Tall Snatch bar(4x3)

  2. Snatch Extension + Snatch + Drop Snatch 60x1 65x1 70(2x1) 75x1 80x1

    • A snatch extension is a snatch pull that is complete as soon as your legs are fully extended (quads and glutes flexed, heels still on the ground or barely off the ground)
    • After your legs have finished their job, leave your arms unactivated / relaxed, i.e., do not use your arms or any part of your upper body to lift the bar further as with a high pull
    • If you are aggresive enough with your legs during the extension, you will generate excess upward energy that should be dispersed by allowing your shoulders to rise upwards. This will appear externally like a shrug, but it is a passive action rather than a deliberate lifting of the shoulders via flexing / contracting your traps. Your traps should not contribute to the upward energy of the bar; your shoulders just go along for the ride.
    • Any time you do a snatch movement, it is beneficial to sit in the catch 3-5 seconds before recovering. You will gain more out of each rep and progress more quickly this way.

  3. Clean + Jerk + Back Jerk 60x2 65x2 70x2 75(2x2) 80x2

  4. Back Squat 65x4 70x3 75x3 80x3 85x2

  5. 4 rounds:

    • Single Leg Abalakov Jump x10
    • Lu Raises x10

  6. Weighted Planks 3x1 minute

Thursday

  1. Snatch 60x3 65(2x3) 70x3 75x3 80x3 + 3-4 heavier singles as desired

    • Any time you do a snatch movement, it is beneficial to sit in the catch 3-5 seconds before recovering. You will gain more out of each rep and progress more quickly this way.

  2. Tall Clean bar(4x3)

    • Warm up your sense of minimum bar height to get under the clean, and the feeling of dropping & catching low.

  3. C&J 60x3 65(2x3) 70x3 75x3 80(2x3) + 3-4 heavier singles as desired

  4. Back Squat 60(3x5) 65(2x5) 70x2 75x2 80x2 85x2 90x1 95(2x1)

  5. 4 rounds:

    • Wide Grip Pullup x5-10
    • Plank Bunny Hop x8
