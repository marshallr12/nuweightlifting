Week 2 of Mesocycle 2

Competition Program

Coaches' Notes
  • If you have athletes that PR during the macrocycle (including at a meet), they should continue to calculate their relative intensities based on their PR's at the beginning of the cycle. The pattern of volume and intensity as the macrocycle progresses already presumes that athletes will improve their strength and conditioning relative to when they started.
  • If you calculate intensities based on a new PR before the macrocyle ends, the athlete will tend to overtrain and regress.
  • New PR's should be used at the start of the next cycle.

Day 4

Monday, Wednesday, Friday, or Saturday

  1. Snatch Grip Push Press Behind the Neck 6x2

    • Hold the lockout position a few seconds before lowering.
    • A slight cheat with your legs is acceptable to get the bar moving.

  2. Clean Grip Upright Row 6x2

    • Keep your chest open
    • Try to keep your elbows directly above your wrists
    • If your elbows start to go backwards, you're lifting the bar too high given your mobility.
    • Most people will only be able to lift the bar properly up to their sternums.

  3. Eccentric Snatch Deadlift 15 secs 85(2x3) 90(2x3)

    • Start in the halting position (bar touching your hips, knees slightly bent, and shoulders forward so that the rear of your armpit is directly above the bar).
    • Lower the bar exactly the reverse of the method you would use to lift it in a snatch:
      • Keep your entire foot flat
      • Keep your center of gravity in the balls of your feet, or mid-foot, depending on the method you've been taught.
      • Use your toes actively (especially press your big to into the ground) to keep yourself from shifting further forward.
      • Once the bar is below your knees, keep your back angle exactly the same and just squat until the bar reaches the ground.

  4. 4 rounds:

    • Banded side plank clamshells x10
    • Calf raise (seated machine if possible) x15-18
