Week 1 of 3-week Conditioning Mesocycle

Competition Program

Coaches' Notes
  • 3-weeks of conditioning to increase weightlifting work capacity.
  • The volume will decrease each week, so the first week is the most taxing.
Athlete Notes
  • 3 weeks of high volume training
  • You will be exhausted at times until your body adjusts. Do the specified volume anyway, but make adjustments if necessar as noted below.
  • The first week is the most difficult since the volume is the highest and your body is the least prepared for it. The good thing is that at the start of a cyle, you should not have any accumulated fatigue from the previous weeks.
  • Do not add any additional exercises / volume to your week or you will lose your gains.
  • The intensity is mostly low, but you will feel the effect of the previous workouts on each subsequent workout, so even the lighter weights may feel heavy.
  • In general, you will need to learn to distiguish between the pain of soreness and the pain of injury. Never work through injury (although ask your coach for an alternative to work around it if possible.
  • Dealing with failed reps

    During high volume training, failing to complete a rep isn't unusual, but you must adapt the movement to be able to complete the specified volume. The number of reps you perform always takes priority over the intensity (amount of weight). So to complete the required reps, use the following strategy:

    • If you get to the point where you cannot perform any of the reps, lower the intensity 5-10%.
    • If you feel like you cannot do another snatch, do a snatch pull instead.
    • If you feel like you cannot do another jerk, do a jerk drive instead.
    • If you feel like you cannot do another clean, do a clean pull instead.
    • Missed reps count towards the volume, so don't repeat a missed rep unless you Clarked it and feel like you didn't expend any energy doing so.

Sunday

  1. PS w/straps 60x5 64x5 66x5 67x5 69x5

    • Slide your feet out on each rep to the same width you would in a full snatch
    • Come to a complete stop in the catch before recovering.

  2. Power Jerk behind the neck 61x4 64x4 66x4 69x4 70x4 69x5

    • Slide your feet out on each rep to the same width you would in a clean.
    • Come to a complete stop in the catch before recovering.

  3. Back Squat 60(2x5) 65(2x4) 70x3 75x3 80(2x3)

  4. Snatch pulls with straps 60x5 65x5 68x5 71x5 73x5

  5. 3 rounds

    • Calf raises 3x15
    • Planks 3x1 minute

Tuesday

  1. Power Clean with straps 61x4 64x4 66x4 69x4 70x4 69x5

    • Come to a compete stop in the catch before recovering.

  2. Power Jerk behind the neck 61x4 64x4 66x4 69x4 70x4 69x5

    • Come to a compete stop in the catch before recovering.

  3. Front Squat 60x5 65x5 70x4 75x3

    • Try to time it so you bounce up a little on each rep.

  4. 3 rounds

    • Reverse Bench Plank x1 minute
    • Pinch grip plate hold x1 minute
    • Bent over dumbbell rows x10

Thursday

  1. Snatch 60x3 65(2x3) 70x3 75(2x3) 80(2x3)

    • Come to a complete stop in the catch of each rep.
    • If you hold the catch 3-5 seconds, you will gain more from each rep and you will improve more rapidly over time.

  2. C&J 60x3 65(2x3) 70x3 75(2x3) 80(2x3)

    • Try to time your catch so you bounce out of the clean immediately.

  3. Back Squat 60(2x4) 65x4 70x3 75(2x3) 80(2x3) 85(2x2)

  4. Abalakov Jump 4x10

  5. Planks 3x1 minute
