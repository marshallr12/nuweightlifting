Week 1 of Mesocycle 2

General Program

Coaches' Notes
  • Sunday is a very long workout, so encourage people to finish their warmups before the session starts, and to switch athletes efficiently between turns.
  • Thursday is a long workout too, although not as long as Sunday's workout.
  • Athletes who miss Sunday's workout due to the competition should perform that workout on Tuesday instead. The originally programmed workout for Tuesday should be skipped / discarded. If they are still feeling trashed after the meet by Tuesday, they can do the originally programmed workout for Tuesday.
  • Missed lifts: generally should not be repeated because they still cause significant fatigue, so still count towards the volume. Repeating a missed lift might be ok when: (a) the intensity is <= 55%, (b) the bar didn't get past the athlete's knees, e.g., "Clarked". A missed lift can cause psychological fatigue even when the intensity is low or the athlete "Clarked," so the default action should be to count the lift towards the volume unless you observe that the athlete seems unfazed by the miss, in which case repeating a missed attempt may be OK.
Athlete Notes
  • Very high volume. This is the most difficult week of the Macrocycle due to the combination of high volume and high intensity.
  • Reduced accessory work due to the high volume of weightlifting movements.
  • Sunday's workout is the most difficult workout of the Macrocycle. Not everyone will be able to complete it as programmed.
  • You will probably be unable to complete the workout if you did not eat well or sleep well before training, or did other athletics last week.

Sunday

  1. Hang PS Above Knee + Hang PS Below Knee + PS from floor + Overhead Squat 5x3 at least 50% RPI and increase 3-5% each set only if the movement is technically as sound as the previous set's intensity.

  2. Power Clean + PJ + 2 behind the neck PJ 60x3 65x3 70x3 75x3 80(2x2)

    • "x3" = PC + PJ + BJ + BJ + PC + PJ + BJ + BJ + PC + PJ + BJ + BJ = Three power cleans and nine power jerks in the set.
    • @65%, you may already start wondering if you're capable of doing much more. Try to ignore those thoughts and keep a "wait and see" attitude.
    • If you are certain you cannot complete a clean, pull it instead.
    • If you are certain you cannot complete a jerk, perform a jerk drive instead.
    • If you cannot properly complete more than one rep on a set, reduce the weight 5-10% on the next set. Finishing the programmed volume is much more important than finishing at the programmed intensity.
    • Reduce the weight 5-10% if you feel like you cannot perform the set safely.

  3. Back Squat 60x7 65x6 70x5 75x4 80x3 83(3x2)

  4. Snatch Pull 85(2x3) 90(2x3)

  5. Weighted Planks 3x1 minute

Tuesday

  1. Hang PS above knee + Hang PS below knee + PS from floor 70(2x1) 75x1 80x1 83(2x1)

  2. Back Jerk 60x4 65x3 70x3 75x3 80(2x3)

  3. Front Squat 70x5 75x5 80(2x3) 85x3 87x3

  4. Halting snatch deadlift 15 secs 85(2x2) 90(2x2)

    • Brush your leg lightly with the bar from the floor to your hips.
    • Pause with the bar at your hips 15 secs
      • Check that your entire foot (including your toes and heels) is on the ground with your center of gravity is in the ball to middle of your feet.
      • Check that your arms are relaxed / unflexed
      • Check that your chest is open and back tight

  5. Weighted Reverse Bench Plank 3x1 minute

Thursday

  1. Snatch 60x3 65x3 70x3 75x3 80x3 85x2 90x2 + 2 singles >= 90%

  2. C&J 60x3 65x3 70x3 75x3 80x2 85x2 90x2 + 2 singles >= 90%

  3. Back Squat 50x6 55x5 60x5 65x4 70x3 75x3 80x3 85x2 90x1 95x1 100x1

  4. Snatch Pull 85(2x3) 90(2x3)

  5. Weighted Planks 3x1 minute
