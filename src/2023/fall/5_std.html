Week 2 Mesocycle 1

General Program

Coaches' Notes
  • Watch for failed attempts on the classic or power lifts. Athletes who fail on the 2nd rep due to fatigue should either decrease the weight on the next set (5-10%) on at least not increase the weight.
Athlete Notes
  • 24% Lower volume that last week, but the intensity is higher.
  • If you feel weak, you are still recovering from last week's volume. This is normal.
  • Since you may not know your 1 rep max, choose weights that allow you to perform the movement correctly. Always prioritize angles / timing / technique over speed and power. Generally, use weights that are a 5-7 in difficulty on the first rep of each set. Use a scale of 1-10, where 1="I'm Captain America - I could do this all day" and 10="I could possibly Hulk smash 1 rep at most." On basic strength movements like squat, you can go heavier if you feel like it.

Sunday

  1. Snatch Pull to hips + 5 secs pause + Snatch High Pull + Power Snatch 60(2x1) 65(2x1) 70(2x1) 75x1

    • Make sure when you are paused with the bar at your hips, your center of gravity is somewhere between the balls of your feet and the middle of your feet: avoid holding your center of gravity in your heels, i.e., counter balancing. It helps to keep your toes actively "grabbing" the ground with a slight curl inside of your shoes.
    • When you are holding the bar at your hips, your arms should still be completely passive / relaxed / unbent.
    • Try to reproduce the same mechanic as your snatch pull to hips inside of your high pull.
    • Try to reproduce the same mechanic as your high pull inside of your power snatch.

  2. Clean Grip Overhead Partial OverHead Squat + Drop PJ bar(4x3)

    • This exercise will help you find the feel of yielding with your legs to complete a jerk rather than relying entirely on pressing the weight up
    • With a clean grip, press the bar from behind your neck until your arms are fully extended for the start position of the overhead squat
    • Maintain the position of the bar overhead, and then squat down about 20-30% of your height
    • Stand back up, lower the bar to behind your neck, then press the bar up behind your neck until it is a little above your head. Hold the bar fixed in that position.
    • Quickly drop under the bar until your arms are fully extended. When you drop under, the bar musn't rise higher than the starting fixed position. In other words, think of pressing yourself down rather than pressing the bar up.
    • As you drop under, your feet should slide out to the catch width for a clean, and no wider.

  3. PC + PJ + Partial Overhead Squat + PJ Behind the Neck 60x3 65x3 70x2 75x2 80(2x2)

    • Each rep is composed of one clean + partial overhead squat + two power jerks (one front, one behind), so you will do six jerks on a set of three reps.
    • On each jerk drive, make sure your have fully extended at the knee joint by forcefully flexing your quads and glutes, then immediately relax and rebend your knees. Do not wait until your are on your "tippy toes" to rebend your knees; you don't need to be a ballerina "en pointe."
    • There should be no point in the jerk where your legs stay motionless (extended) while your arms continue to extend (press) the bar up. Any moment that your arms are extending, your legs should still be rebending.
    • Since the use of gravity is "free" energy, learning to drop under the bar means you will be able to complete more reps without tiring out and pressing out. However, dropping under can be psychologically difficult because one's instinct under stress is to push harder, rather than yield more.
    • Instinct can be overcome by correctly practicing a movement with enough repetition while fatigued. When fatigued, you can't "think things through," so the motor pattern you practice trains your autonomic nervous system, which is responsible for reflexes.
    • On each rep, try to find the feeling of yielding your legs while you extend your arms. Even if you aren't successful, your intention will eventually produce results after much practice and fatigue.
