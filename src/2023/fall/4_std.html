Week 1 of 3-week Conditioning Mesocycle

Competition Program

Coaches' Notes
  • 3-weeks of conditioning to increase weightlifting work capacity.
  • The volume will decrease each week, so the first week is the most taxing.
Athlete Notes
  • 3 weeks of high volume training
  • You will be exhausted at times until your body adjusts
  • The first week is the most difficult since the volume is the highest and your body is the least prepared for it. The good thing is that at the start of a cyle, you should not have any accumulated fatigue from the previous weeks.
  • The intensity is mostly low, but you will feel the effect of the previous workouts on each subsequent workout, so even the lighter weights may feel heavy.
  • In general, you will need to learn to distinguish between the pain of soreness and the pain of injury. Never work through injury (although ask your coach for an alternative to work around it if possible.
  • Since you may not know your 1 rep max, choose weights that allow you to perform the movement correctly. Always prioritize angles / timing / technique over speed and power. Generally, use weights that are a 5-7 in difficulty on the first rep of each set. Use a scale of 1-10, where 1="I'm Captain America - I could do this all day" and 10="I could possibly Hulk smash 1 rep at most." On basic strength movements like squat, you can go heavier if you feel like it.

Sunday

  1. Snatch DL to below knees + Snatch DL to above knees + Snatch DL to hips + 2 Snatch high pull = 1 rep 5x1

    • Start each portion of the rep from the ground.
    • Keep you center of gravity stable (wherever you started - ball to mid foot) throughout the entire movement.
    • Lightly brush your thighs as soon as the bar passes your knees.
    • Keep the bar close (brush your shirt on the way up) after your hips rather than allowing it to bounce away from your hips.

  2. 3 PJ behind the neck + 2 Pause Split Jerk behind neck 5 secs 5x1

    • Come to a complete stop in the catch and pose for a moment before recovering from each power jerk.
    • Try to slide your feet out in the PJ to the same width you'd do an overhead squat.
    • Try to stabilize the bar when holding the split for 5 seconds before you recover.
    • Recover by stepping the front foot back first, then the rear foot forward.
    • Keep holding the bar overhead until you have recovered.
    • Lower the bar to your back after the jerk: this requires technique too.

  3. Tempo Pause Overhead Squat 5 secs 5x5

    • If you are feeling mobile, you can experiment with a narrower grip.
    • Sit at the bottom of the squat and stabilize for 5 seconds.

  4. Back Squat 60(2x5) 65(2x4) 7x3 75x3 80(2x3)

    • If you don't know your max, use a scale of 1-10.

  5. 3 rounds

    • Barbell Rows x8
    • Wrist Curls x15
    • Planks x1 minute

Tuesday

  1. Clean DL to below knee + Clean DL to above knee + Clean DL to upper thigh + Clean High Pull = 1 rep 5x1

    • Start each portion of the rep from the ground.
    • Keep you center of gravity stable (wherever you started - ball to mid foot) throughout the entire movement.
    • Lightly brush your thighs as soon as the bar passes your knees.
    • At the top of the pull, allow your elbows to flare out to disperse the energy you created with your legs.
    • No shrugging

  2. 2 PJ behind the neck + 3 Pause Split Jerk behind neck 5 secs 5x1

    • (see Sunday's notes)

  3. Front Squat 60x5 65x5 70x4 75x3

    • If you don't know your max, use a scale of 1-10.

  4. 3 rounds:

    • Dumbell Bench Press x1
    • Reverse Bench Plank x1 minute

  5. Tempo Snatch 5x3

Thursday

  1. Snatch DL to below knee + Snatch DL to above knee + Snatch DL to hip + 2 Snatch = 1 rep 5x1

    • Come to a complete stop in the catch of each snatch before you recover rather than bouncing up.
    • Your snatch technique will improve more quickly if you pause in the catch 3-5 seconds before recovering.

  2. 3 PJ behind the neck + 2 Pause Split Jerk behind neck 5 secs 5x1

  3. Back Squat 50x6 60(2x5) 65x5 70x4 75(2x3) 80(2x3)

  4. Abalakov Jump 4x10

  5. Tempo Snatch bar(5x3)

  6. 3 rounds

    • Reverse Wrist Curls 3x15
    • Planks x1 minute
