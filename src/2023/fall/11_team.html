Week 11 of 3-week Pre-competition Mesocycle

Team Program

Coaches' Notes
  • Sunday is heavy day so we can sync up with the Sunday meet day.
  • We are mostly focused on the classical lifts, so there is not much variety in the exercise selection.
  • The purpose of going from 87% to 70% to 90% is to simulate a long wait between competition platform attempts. The athlete has to get comfortable with a long wait between the last heavy attempt at 87 and will need to focus on reproducing the same technique on the 90% attempt as the prior 70% attempt.
  • When you don't practice taking lighter attempts to stay warm during long waits between attempts on the competition platform, you will have to take heavy pulls or heavy snatches / C&J's instead. The latter method has the advantage of keeping the athlete neurally primed for the competition attempts due to the specificity of the weights, without having to do any special training during the macrocycle, but at the expensive of extra competition fatigue which will reduce power output. Athletes who train for large jumps during their macrocycles will be able to stay neurally primed for long waits and maintain both technique and power once it is their turn on the platform.
Athlete Notes
  • Volume is very low and intensity is high.
  • This week fatigue is deliberately reduced as you are peaking for meet day.
  • Do not add additional reps / exercises even if you feel like you can do more. That includes non-weightlifting related activities outside of your team workouts.
  • You may feel that the weights & volume are not challenging enough to feel like you got a good workout. This is expected. Avoid the temptation to add more to your workouts to get the post-workout high which is normally the result of strenuously exerting yourself.
  • When you perform the 70% lifts that come just before the 90% lifts, focus on producing technically perfect lifts. When you perform the 90% lifts, approach the bar exactly the same way and reproduce the movements you did @70%. At this point in the cycle, the main challenge of a 90% lift isn't power or strength, but being able to consistently use the same technique you use for lighter weights.
